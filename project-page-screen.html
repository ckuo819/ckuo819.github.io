 Designing Santa Clara University’s First Rapid-Response Student Feedback System

What I Build?

We built a protocol for launching Santa Clara University's first-ever Pulse Survey, including the design, administration, and analysis process. This initiative aimed to foster better communication between students and the university by capturing real-time student feedback on pressing issues.

Timeline: January 2024 to June 2024
Project/Client: SCU Institutional Research Pulse Survey Committee

Impact

Designed and executed the university’s first Pulse Survey targeting student belonging and mental wellbeing .
Enabled the Office of Institutional Research to quickly capture and analyze student insights to inform timely decisions .
Established a repeatable, scalable framework for future quarterly surveys, potentially expanding to graduate students, faculty, and staff.
Increased student engagement by providing a space to voice needs and experiences, and encouraged trust through transparent reporting.

Identifying Problems

Prior to this project, SCU lacked a rapid-response mechanism for understanding student needs on an ongoing basis. Traditional surveys were infrequent and often too slow to address timely student concerns , leading to:
Missed opportunities to enhance the student experience in real time
Gaps in understanding student wellbeing and belonging
Limited actionable data for university leadership

Topic Selection : Focused on “Belonging” and “Mental Wellbeing” based on institutional priorities.
 Questionnaire Design : Created a concise survey (≤12 questions), using validated instruments such as NSSE and PHQ-4, ensuring benchmarking and consistency.
 Administration Planning : Scheduled launch for Week 4 of Spring 2024 quarter; included pre-announcements and reminders to maximize response rate.
 Execution : Distributed the survey to all undergraduate students via email.
 Analysis & Reporting : Analyzed responses using SPSS and shared findings with institutional stakeholders and on the SCU website.
 Future-Proofing : Developed documentation and protocols for future implementation; recommended A/B testing and platform expansion.

Approach

Before the Pulse Survey, SCU lacked a quick and consistent way to gather student feedback. Traditional surveys were long, infrequent, and often too slow to inform real-time decisions. Students also felt disengaged, unsure if their responses led to change. To solve this, we designed short (1–5 minute) surveys focused on timely, relevant topics. We used validated scales for consistency and tracked changes over time. Strategic reminders and transparent reporting helped build trust and increase participation. Clear consent and confidentiality measures ensured students felt safe sharing their experiences.

Pain Points and Solution
Reflection

 Leading the Pulse Survey project deepened my skills in UX research, data analysis (SPSS) , and institutional collaboration . It also reinforced the value of empathetic design —listening to users (students) and translating their voices into actionable institutional insights . Creating a protocol from scratch taught me how to balance research rigor with real-world constraints like time, engagement, and accessibility. Most importantly, I learned how data-driven practices can meaningfully improve the student experience.
