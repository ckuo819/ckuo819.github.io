Project 1

Enhancing the Workday Academic Platform Experience

UX Research
Usability Testing
Interaction Design
Timeline
4 months (Fall 2023)
Client
Santa Clara University
Role
UX Researcher
Impact
Recommendations adopted by SCU IT to fix search and improve navigation

The Problem: A Confusing & Inefficient Platform

SCU's Workday platform was causing significant student frustration due to vague categories, an inefficient 10-click process for core tasks, and an ineffective search function.

Pain Point 1: Confusing Information Architecture

This visual demonstrates the confusing user flow. (1) Starting from the dashboard, (2) the user must guess from vague top-level categories, (3) only to be met with another list of overlapping and non-descriptive sub-categories, increasing the number of clicks needed to complete a task.

Step 1: The journey begins. A student lands on the main dashboard needing to find a specific academic task, such as planning their courses.

Step 2: The first obstacle. After opening the menu, the student is met with four vague, high-level categories. They are forced to guess which one contains their goal.

Step 3: Deeper confusion. Choosing 'Academics' reveals yet another list of confusing and overlapping sub-categories. Terms like 'Planning & Registration' and 'Academic Records' are not self-evident, leading to more clicks and uncertainty.

Pain Point 2: The 10-Click User Journey

This video demonstrates the frustrating 10-click process required to perform a simple task - students had to navigate through multiple layers of confusing menus to accomplish basic academic tasks.

Pain Point 3: Ineffective Search Function

When searching for a critical term like "final" (expecting exam schedules), the system returned unrelated results, including a student's name. This demonstrated a core failure in the search logic and created a confusing user experience.

My Approach: A Mixed-Methods Study

To ground our solutions in real user data, I used a mixed-methods approach to understand both the "what" and the "why" behind student frustrations.

Qualitative Data

I conducted 9 in-depth user interviews to gather rich, contextual stories about student experiences, which revealed the nuances of their struggles with navigation.

Quantitative Data

We designed and distributed a survey to gather broad insights, receiving over 120 responses which helped us quantify the most common pain points.

Synthesis & Analysis

We used affinity mapping to cluster the qualitative feedback and analyzed survey data in Qualtrics. This synthesis clearly pointed to the navigation system as the core issue to focus on.

The Solution: A Multi-faceted Approach to Clarity

To address the identified pain points, we proposed a set of targeted solutions to improve clarity and efficiency across the platform's navigation and search systems.

Solution 1: Improving Vertical Navigation

To solve the confusing navigation, we recommended clarifying the link names and adding helpful descriptions, as shown here. These changes were based on our proposal to use card-sorting to understand how students mentally group these categories.

Solution 2: Enhancing Horizontal Navigation

A 'Save Progress' feature ensures students don't have to re-enter common filters repeatedly.

A collapsible sidebar menu allows for quicker navigation and reduces page-to-page clicks.

Solution 3: A Smarter, More Consistent Search

The search bar should give relevant suggestions and improved results for ambiguous terms.

Autofill suggestions should match existing, clearly-labeled subcategories to improve discoverability.

Reflection

This project reinforced the importance of focusing on core user frustrations. By systematically identifying navigation as the primary pain point, we were able to propose targeted, high-impact solutions that the SCU IT team could readily implement, leading to tangible improvements for the student body.
