Workday UX Research
Improving Santa Clara University Workday Portal Using UX Research Methods

What I Build?

Identify the pain points of academic platform of Workday navigation system and suggestions on how to improve them.

Timeline: from September 2023 to December 2023
Project/Client: Santa Clara University class project

Impact

Suggestions to the IT team that works on the academic workday system in SCU and they fix some of the issues such as search engines error in the websites and keeping working on make the websites navigation more smoothly.

Identifying Problems

Categories aren't Self-Evident: students are confused what the categories means and couldn't find the exact things they want efficiently.
Long Navigation Process: it takes students 10 steps (clicks) enable them to find the courses they want to add in their course plan.
Search Bar: It couldn't find things correctly and give you results that is irrelevant.

Approach

Data collection: Quantitative: surveys, n=120; Qualitative: interviews, n=9
Data analysis: Qualtrics and Google Excel
Solution
Vertical Navigation: using Follow-up surveys / card-sorting question; Improve similar and overlapping naming; Include description for each feature.
Horizontal Navigation: create a 'save progress' feature, which ensure students don't need to click through a hassle of buttons to get to what are used the most in the Workday system.
Useful Sidebar: The original sidebar only contains 4 categories. We suggests to create a collapsible sidebar menu can help students locate things quickly, and reducing the time of navigation.
Search Bar Improvement: the search bar needs to gives students relevant search suggestions. And we suggest to also match the autofill search suggestions with the existing subcategories.

Pain Points and Solution

Since the workday system in Santa Clara university's students portal are newly launch when we decide to do this project, we got lots of complain and issues when we conducted the interviews. And we struggle to pain points the main struggles that we want to focus on. After discussed with our professor, we decided to use post notes to categorize and organize all the pain points that reflected from our experience and from the interviews and find out the biggest categories and focus on that, which is the navigation in workday student portal system.

Reflection

One thing is that we all agreed that it would have been really interesting to have also collected data from SCU's staff as they also use Workday. We would want to investigate whether they have the same problems as students do using workday or if their problems are entirely different and how so. We also reflected on the possibly of redoing this project and one thing we would've have done differently is to try our hand at card sorting ourselves. To briefly define what card sorting is, it's where we would ask users to organize cards into groups that they feel is appropriate to them. We would do this with the categories and subcategories to get a clear picture of how students would organize the categories themselves for ourselves and we'd then present that information. Additionally we'd like to point out that during our process of researching, workday has been updating some of its platform features. For example, in the academics tab they added a whole new box for registration requirements.
