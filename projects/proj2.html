Project 2

Launching SCU's Inaugural Student Pulse Survey

Program Design
Data Analysis (SPSS)
Survey Methodology
Timeline
6 months (Spring 2023)
Client
Santa Clara University & SCU Institutional Research
Role
Lead Researcher & Protocol Designer
Impact
Established a repeatable framework for rapid student feedback and institutional decision-making

The Problem: A Communication Gap Between Students and the University

Prior to this project, SCU lacked a rapid-response mechanism for understanding student needs. Traditional annual surveys were too slow, leading to missed opportunities to enhance the student experience, gaps in understanding student well-being, and a lack of timely data for leadership. The solution required a system built on four key principles:

The Core Principles Guiding Our New Rapid Feedback System

Brief

Quick to design, complete, and analyze.

Intentional

Actionable purpose beyond curiosity.

Transparent

Publishing data and acting on it.

Consistent

Bench-marking questions for tracking.

Our Approach: Building a Repeatable Protocol

We designed a comprehensive and repeatable protocol from the ground up. Our process was designed to be rigorous, respectful of students' time, and actionable for the institution, focusing on topic selection, questionnaire design, and strategic administration.

Our Four-Phase Framework

Decide on Topic
→
Design Survey
→
Distribution
→
Analyze & Report

Our four-phase framework ensured a structured approach, from identifying priorities to delivering reports.

The Solution: A Scalable Framework for Student Feedback

The core of our solution was not just a single survey, but a complete, future-proofed protocol that addressed the primary pain points of the old system: survey fatigue and student disengagement. The final protocol established a repeatable and scalable cycle, turning student feedback from an annual event into an ongoing institutional habit.

Survey Implementation and Results

The final survey deliverable, designed to be completed in under 5 minutes using validated scales like the PHQ-4 and National Survey of Student Engagement (NSSE).

Reflection

Leading this project from scratch deepened my skills in UX research, data analysis using SPSS, and institutional collaboration. It reinforced the value of empathetic design—listening to users and translating their voices into actionable institutional insights. Creating a protocol from scratch taught me how to balance research rigor with real-world constraints like time and engagement.
