UCLA Community Based Research
Partnership with 826LA organizations

What I Build?

A comprehensive evaluation of 826LA's Field Trip Program through ethnographic observation, interviews, and demographic analysis to assess its educational impact and community alignment. Our team produced a detailed final report, including a customized Theory of Change and evidence-based program recommendations.

Timeline: January 2025 to June 2025
Project/Client: 826LA

Impact

Highlighted how experiential and collaborative storytelling fosters student creativity and confidence.
Uncovered gaps in outreach and post-trip engagement, leading to concrete recommendations adopted for future programming.
Proposed a site-specific feedback tool to improve localized evaluation and program planning.

Identifying Problems

While 826LA's Field Trip Program aimed to support students from underserved communities through creative writing experiences, there was a lack of data on long-term learning outcomes, equitable access across school demographics, and alignment with shifting neighborhood populations.

Approach

Qualitative: Conducted 3 field observations and 9 semi-structured interviews with staff and teachers.
Quantitative: Analyzed demographic data from LA planning reports and school economically disadvantaged rates to assess program reach.
Theoretical Frameworks: Applied collaborative learning, experiential education, and Theory of Change models to guide analysis.

Data visualization (Quant): View Spreadsheet
Presentation: View Presentation

Pain Points and Solution

Pain Point: Limited follow-up activities to sustain learning after trips.
Solution: Proposed post-trip resources and classroom-aligned extensions like student portfolios.

Pain Point: Uneven school participation due to informal outreach networks.
Solution: Recommended targeted outreach strategies and clarification of logistical support (e.g., transportation).

Pain Point: Aggregated post-trip surveys lacked local insights.
Solution: Advocated for a chapter-specific feedback mechanism.

Reflection

Through this project, I gained invaluable experience in user research and data analysis. I learned how to effectively gather and analyze qualitative data, identify patterns in user behavior, and translate these insights into actionable recommendations. This experience has strengthened my ability to approach problems systematically and create solutions that truly address user needs.
