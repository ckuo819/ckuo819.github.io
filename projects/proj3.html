Evaluating and Enhancing 826LA's Field Trip Program

Program Evaluation
Ethnographic Research
Demographic Analysis

826LA Non-Profit
Role
UX Research & Analyst
Impact
Recommendations adopted to refine outreach and improve program engagement

The Problem: Measuring Impact and Ensuring Equitable Access

While 826LA's Field Trip Program was highly regarded, the organization lacked data to answer critical strategic questions about its long-term learning outcomes, equitable reach across diverse communities, and alignment with shifting neighborhood populations.

Core Findings 1: Uneven Program Reach

While 826LA's Field Trip Program was highly regarded, the organization lacked data to answer critical strategic questions about its long-term learning outcomes, equitable reach across diverse communities, and alignment with shifting neighborhood populations.

Our demographic analysis revealed that while Echo Park programming reached a consistently high-need population, the Mar Vista site served a much more economically diverse group of schools, indicating potential gaps in equitable access.

Core Findings 2: Lack of Sustained Learning

"Teachers expressed strong interest in having more structured support, such as classroom-ready writing prompts, extension activities, or post-trip projects to reinforce the skills and confidence gained during the trip." — Key Finding from Teacher Interviews

Our qualitative research found that the creative spark from the field trip was often lost due to a lack of formal follow-up activities to sustain learning.

My Approach: A Community-Focused Evaluation

To develop a holistic understanding of the program, our team employed a robust research strategy combining qualitative inquiry, quantitative analysis, and established theoretical frameworks to guide our analysis and recommendations.

Qualitative Data

Conducted 3 ethnographic field observations and 9 semi-structured interviews with staff and teachers to gather rich, contextual insights.

Quantitative Data

Analyzed demographic data from LA planning reports and school disadvantaged rates to assess program reach and equity.

Theoretical Framework

Applied a custom-built Theory of Change model to map program activities to intended short-term and long-term outcomes.

The Solution: Actionable, Evidence-Based Recommendations

Our mixed-methods research culminated in a set of evidence-based recommendations designed to enhance the program's impact, improve equity, and guide future development.

Recommendations from Qualitative Data about Field Trip Program

Deepen Teacher Engagement

Enhance pre-trip communication to inquire about student writing levels and teacher interest in follow-up activities, ensuring better instructional alignment.

Streamline Logistics

Proactively clarify and communicate the availability of transportation support to reduce logistical barriers for under-resourced schools.

Implement Localized Feedback

Develop a chapter-specific feedback tool to capture more relevant, timely, and actionable insights for local program planning and iteration.

Recommendations from Demographic Data

A. Prioritize Echo Park Outreach

Continue to prioritize partnerships in Echo Park, where data confirms a consistently high-need student population that aligns with 826LA's core mission.

B. Reassess Mar Vista

In light of gentrification trends, monitor and reassess Mar Vista programming to ensure resources are directed to the areas of greatest need.

C. Guide Resource Allocation

Use the demographic data as a strategic tool to guide future resource allocation, partner recruitment, and program positioning.

A Proposed Theory of Change For the Field Trip Program

A key deliverable was this Theory of Change model, which visually maps program inputs, activities, and outcomes to provide a clear strategic roadmap for future development.

Reflection

This project was a formative experience in applying research skills to a real-world community need. It strengthened my ability to synthesize qualitative and quantitative data into a cohesive story and translate those insights into actionable recommendations that truly address user and community needs.
